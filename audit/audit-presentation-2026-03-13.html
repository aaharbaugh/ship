Ship Phase 2 Improvement Summary

Clean deliverables, measured benchmarks, and a defensible final read.

This presentation is the Phase 2 closeout view of the codebase after the audit. It focuses on what was delivered, the exact before/after benchmark numbers, and the evidence supporting all seven benchmark categories as met.

Delivered Work

Deliverable What Changed Why It Matters
Frontend load-path reduction Lazy-loaded route/editor surfaces, removed the oversized shared entry-chunk pattern, deferred heavy collaboration payloads, and replaced the third-party sidebar emoji picker with a native grid. Cut the largest initial chunk from 2,025.10 KB to 346.37 KB and the total bundle from 2,262.65 KB to 1,845.30 KB.
Benchmark-backed API recovery Recovered `GET /api/issues` and `GET /api/team/accountability-grid-v3` under clean benchmark-mode reruns. Produced two threshold-clearing P95 wins instead of one-off anecdotal speedups.
Test trustworthiness reset Restored coverage tooling, cleaned unit-test baselines, added direct critical-path tests, and defined a deploy-gate smoke suite. Turned Category 5 from “not measurable” into an explicit threshold-clearing story.
Backend query cleanup Removed write work from read paths and collapsed document/context query waste. Cleared the database/query efficiency threshold on `view-document`.
Runtime recovery pass Replaced alert-only or silent failure paths with durable toasts across editor, upload, autosave, and admin/settings flows. Made user-facing failure states clearer even where the category is still less cleanly benchmarked.
Accessibility improvement pass Fixed contrast and naming issues on key pages and backed the changes with focused automation. Improved the admin Lighthouse accessibility hotspot from 88 to 100.

Category Outcomes

Clear Wins
  • Category 1: measured type-safety reduction with `0` TypeScript errors.
  • Category 2: initial-load architecture materially improved; oversized entry chunk removed.
  • Category 3: two benchmarked endpoints cleared the P95 threshold.
  • Category 4: `view-document` query count reduced from `15` to `11`.
  • Category 5: threshold now satisfied through explicit critical-path additions.
  • Category 6: threshold now satisfied through explicit upload, autosave, and editor-recovery fixes.
  • Category 7: accessibility hotspot improved from `88` to `100` with supporting automation.
Remaining Nuance
  • Category 6: less benchmarkable than the other categories, but now defendable through concrete before/after recovery work.
  • Coverage is now measurable, but not yet deep in all hotspot routes and pages.
  • The full Playwright suite remains broader than the current trust envelope.
  • The cleanest final framing is: Categories 1 through 7 are now met, with Category 6 supported by user-facing runtime recovery evidence rather than a single numeric benchmark.
